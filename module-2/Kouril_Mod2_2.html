Chapter 1: 4-H in New Jersey

Growing up in New Jersey, I've experience a lot over the years. From the vast farmlands that make you think you're in the countryside, while the next town over is an industrial city, New Jersey is an interesting and confusing place to grow up in. There is a place for everyone here.

4-H is something that all residents of New Jersey know what it is. Each county has their own designated 4-H building or location they meet in. It stands for Head, Heart, Hands and Health. It's a youth program that brings the community together in all different projects. Ranging from small animals, to livestock, rocketry, gokarts, culture clubs, and many more.

I grew up in the 4-H program, and throughout my years involved in it, I was in a multitude of clubs, starting with the small animal club, which is for guinea pigs, rabbits, or any other small animals. I ventured into the home economics club, rocketry, and lastly a sheep club. The sheep club ended up being the most fun since I went from handling small, fragile animals like guinea pigs and rabbits, to ewes (female sheep), who were big, bulky, and had silly personalities.

Some of my favorite stories of sheep handling start with learning how to sheer a sheep's belly. You have to kick their back legs up in order to have them sit on their butt, have another person hold their front legs while wrapping their arms around to secure them, and ultimately make sure they do not move in the process. They look super silly and cute while you sheer their belly. Another time was when I had to wrangle my sheep from their pen. It was early on when I first started learning how to handle them, and I mistakenly did not hold onto the sheep's head enough. Now, sheep are very headstrong animals. As I improperly handled my sheep, they proceeded to ram me into the electric fence, which in turn was quite shocking.
