A Story About New Jersey's Pine Barrens

People of New Jersey say the Pine Barens are quiet at night, always too quiet. Down past the old cranberry bogs, there's a place locals will never go after dark - Lantern Man's Crossing.

Years ago, and of course, no one ever agrees on when, there was a railroad worker named Joseph tending to the old Pine Barrens line. He walked the tracks every night with a __________ lantern, checking for damage, clearing branches, and making sure the trains could pass safely.

One stormy night, the wind howled so loud that no one heard the train coming. Not even Joseph.

They say the conductor saw a flash of light on the tracks - Joseph's lantern - and then, nothing.

The following morning, the lantern was found, but no sign of Joseph.

The railroad shutdown that section of the track, but the legend didn't. That's because people started seeing a light in the woods.

Not a flashlight.

Not a campfire.

A lantern - old, metal, swinging slowly back and forth.

Some say it appears when you're lost.

Some say the light glows with a strange, __________ color.

Some say it appears when you're somewhere you shouldn't be.

And some say it appears when you speak Joseph's name out loud.

But, here's the part that makes people nervous:

If you follow the lantern, it always stays the same distance ahead of you.

Never closer.

Never farther.

Just leading you deeper into the pines.

And if you stop following it...

The lantern stops too.

Then it turns toward you.

Some people swear they hear footsteps behind them, slow and __________.

That's when you see him - a tall figure, face hidden in the shadows, holding the lantern out like an invitation.

Witnesses say he doesn't speak. He just tilts his head, like he's waiting for you to take the light from his hand.

But if you do?

You're never to be seen again.

The only thing people ever find is the __________ - burning bright, swinging gently, waiting for the next traveler to follow.

If you ever find yourself driving through the Pine Barrens at night, and you see a warm yellow glow drifting between the trees...

Don't stop.

Don't look.

And whatever you do...

Don't follow the light.
